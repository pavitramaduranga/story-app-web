# BabooStories Core Concepts

This document captures the main theme and content principles for BabooStories. Use it as a quick reference when creating, reviewing, or updating website and app content.

## Main Theme

BabooStories is a calm, kid- and toddler-friendly story collection for parents and caregivers to read with their children. It is especially useful as a bedtime story collection, but story time can happen whenever a family wants a quiet, connected moment together.

## Origin and Voice

- BabooStories is developed by a solo app developer who is also a parent.
- The stories come from real parent-child reading moments: these are stories the developer has read to their own kid.
- The voice should feel warm, practical, gentle, and parent-to-parent.
- Content should avoid sounding overly corporate, clinical, or fear-based.

## Story Quality and Review

- Each story is reviewed by at least one qualified early educator.
- Content should be appropriate for young children, including toddlers.
- Stories should support calm, age-friendly reading experiences.
- The app should emphasize care, suitability, and thoughtful review without making exaggerated educational or medical claims.

## Audience

- BabooStories is for parents and caregivers.
- Parents use the app to read stories to their kids.
- The product should speak to adults while keeping children’s comfort, attention span, and emotional safety in mind.

## Reading Experience

- Stories should be calm, gentle, and toddler-friendly.
- BabooStories works well for bedtime, wind-down routines, quiet time, and shared family reading.
- The app is not limited to bedtime; it can support any calm story moment during the day.
- Content should encourage connection between parent and child rather than passive consumption.

## Parent Flexibility

- Parents are encouraged to change, act out, shorten, expand, or adapt stories as they see fit.
- Website and app copy should reinforce that there is no single “perfect” way to read a story.
- Parents can follow their child’s mood, curiosity, and needs while reading.
- The app should feel like a supportive story companion, not a strict script.

## Content Alignment Checklist

Before publishing new content, confirm that it:

- Reflects a calm, parent-led story experience.
- Supports toddlers and young children with gentle, age-friendly language.
- Speaks primarily to parents and caregivers.
- Fits the BabooStories origin: built by a solo developer parent from stories read to their own child.
- Mentions educator review accurately when relevant.
- Allows room for parents to adapt stories in their own voice.
- Avoids pressure, guilt, fear-based messaging, or overpromising outcomes.
- Positions bedtime as an important use case without implying it is the only use case.
